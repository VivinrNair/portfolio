LSE · MSc Local Economic Development

Hi, I’m Vivin! 👋

I work where policy meets technology - a sustainability and development professional by trade, a builder by instinct. At UST, a global firm of 30,000+ people, I lead the enterprise sustainability programme - carbon accounting across 30+ countries, science-based targets, and the reporting machinery behind CSRD, CDP and EcoVadis. I keep a close tab on where the rules are heading - CBAM, EUDR, BRSR. That’s the problem I most recently built for: Deltamere, a sustainability engine that tells small ESG teams exactly which regulations apply to them, what changed, and what to do by when.

The habit under all of it is research. I came to sustainability from economic geography - my MSc at LSE examined how governments grow and sustain technology clusters, and I still work the way it taught me: read a problem closely, then write to think it through. That writing runs from an Elsevier book chapter on data-driven urban economies to essays on AI governance and development for the World Economic Forum, published in English and Spanish. Much of my work has lived in and around government at national, state and city level: a fellowship at India’s Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs, where I built LEIP, an economic intelligence platform that helped a city of 3.8 million read its own economy. Outside that, I curate my city’s Global Shapers hub, where we co-founded a health consortium that channelled ₹4M into public hospitals.

Highlights

Build · 2026

Deltamere - an AI sustainability analyst

Inside most mid-caps, one person handles ESG compliance alone. Deltamere reads a company’s profile, filters ~200 frameworks down to the ten that apply, and turns each into dated work - then drafts the disclosures. Conceived, designed and built end-to-end.

Product site 30s animatic

Moment · 2025

Summer Davos, Tianjin

One of 60 Global Shapers worldwide selected for the World Economic Forum’s Annual Meeting of the New Champions - carrying a Global-South view of AI, sustainability and development into the room.

Build & research · 2020–21

LEIP - economic intelligence for Indian cities

Indian cities generate much of the country’s GDP but plan little of their own economies. As an India Smart Cities Fellow I co-built LEIP - cluster mapping, location quotients and a decision-support system - piloted for Thiruvananthapuram’s 3.8 million residents.

Case study Book chapter
